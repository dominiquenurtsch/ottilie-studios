The Cartier Love collection was born from heartbreak, not love

Created by Italian jewellery designer Aldo Cipullo in 1969

When Cartier first opened their flagship store in New York, the brand found itself looking to new designs that moved away from Parisian opulence and towards the quiet luxury of NYC dressing. In 1969, Italian jewellery designer Aldo Cipullo — also the creator of Juste Un Clou — had been dealing with personal heartbreak and wanted to create a piece of jewellery that signified a committed relationship, lasting forever.

The original design required a screwdriver and your loved one to attach the bracelet to your wrist, with the screwdriver being discarded afterwards. Many celebrity couples began to announce their relationships publicly by wearing the iconic bracelet. An urban legend says some surgeons started to keep Cartier screwdrivers handy for this very reason.

In 2025, the Cartier Love collection grows again

Over the next few decades, the Cartier Love ring, the Cartier Love bracelet with diamonds, the Cartier Open Love bracelet (signifying self-love) and the medium model (with a hinge to be opened and closed by yourself) were added to the Love family by the French maison.

The latest additions, the Cartier Love Unlimited bracelet and ring, mark another incredible new chapter. Each Cartier Love Unlimited bracelet is made of 200 components with a brand-first clasp that allows the bracelet to be put on or taken off without the usage of a screwdriver. Love is now unlimited — with the bracelets being able to attach to each other unlimited.
